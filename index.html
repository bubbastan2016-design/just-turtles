🐢 Meet the Sea Turtles

🥬 Green Sea Turtle

Least Concern (2025)
Scientific Name Chelonia mydas
Weight 150-300 lbs
Lifespan 80+ years
Diet Seagrass & Algae

Amazing Fact: Green turtles are the only herbivorous sea turtles! Their name comes from the green fat under their shell, not their shell color.

Conservation Win: In October 2025, green turtles were downlisted from Endangered to Least Concern thanks to decades of protection efforts! Their population has increased 28% since the 1970s.

🖤 Leatherback Sea Turtle

Vulnerable
Scientific Name Dermochelys coriacea
Weight Up to 2,000 lbs
Length Up to 6 feet
Diet Jellyfish

Amazing Fact: Leatherbacks are the largest turtles on Earth! They can dive deeper than 4,000 feet - deeper than any other turtle!

Unique Feature: They don't have a hard shell - instead they have a leathery carapace with ridges!

🧡 Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Vulnerable
Scientific Name Caretta caretta
Weight 200-350 lbs
Shell Length Up to 4 feet
Diet Crustaceans & Conch

Amazing Fact: Loggerheads have powerful jaws that can crush hard-shelled prey like queen conchs!

Migration: They travel thousands of miles across ocean basins during their lives!

💜 Hawksbill Sea Turtle

Critically Endangered
Scientific Name Eretmochelys imbricata
Weight 100-150 lbs
Shell Beautiful pattern
Diet Sea Sponges

Amazing Fact: Hawksbills have a sharp, pointed beak like a hawk - that's how they got their name!

Threat: Their beautiful shells were used for tortoiseshell jewelry, causing population decline. Only about 57,000 remain.

❤️ Kemp's Ridley

Critically Endangered
Scientific Name Lepidochelys kempii
Weight 80-100 lbs
Size Smallest sea turtle
Diet Blue Crabs

Amazing Fact: Kemp's ridleys are the smallest and rarest sea turtles! Only about 10,000 remain.

Nesting: They nest in large groups called "arribadas" - hundreds come ashore together!

🤍 Olive Ridley

Vulnerable
Scientific Name Lepidochelys olivacea
Weight 80-100 lbs
Abundance Most abundant
Diet Fish & Invertebrates

Amazing Fact: Olive ridleys are the most abundant sea turtle species!

Arribadas: Like Kemp's ridleys, they nest in spectacular mass nesting events with thousands of turtles!

💛 Flatback Sea Turtle

Data Deficient
Scientific Name Natator depressus
Weight 200 lbs
Range Australia only
Diet Soft-bodied prey

Amazing Fact: Flatbacks are the only sea turtle species that lives entirely in Australian waters!

Shell: They have a flat, olive-gray shell that gives them their name!

🧠 Did You Know?

  • 🌡️ Temperature decides gender: Warmer nests produce female turtles, cooler nests produce males!
  • 🧭 Natural GPS: Sea turtles can sense Earth's magnetic field to navigate across oceans!
  • 😴 Underwater naps: Sea turtles can hold their breath for up to 5 hours while resting!
  • 🥚 Many eggs: Females lay thousands of eggs in their lifetime, but only 1 in 1,000 hatchlings survives to adulthood!
  • 🦕 Dino buddies: Turtles shared the Earth with dinosaurs over 100 million years ago!
  • 🐢 Shell facts: A turtle's shell is made of over 50 bones fused together - they're literally wearing their bones on the outside!

💚 Be a Turtle Hero!

Sea turtles have survived for over 100 million years, but now they need our help. Here's how YOU can make a difference!

Threats Turtles Face

🎣

Bycatch

Turtles get caught in fishing nets and hooks meant for other fish. Over 250,000 sea turtles are accidentally caught each year!

🏖️

Beach Development

Hotels and lights on beaches confuse hatchlings. They need dark, quiet beaches to find their way to the ocean!

🥤

Plastic Pollution

Turtles mistake plastic bags for jellyfish and eat them. This can make them very sick or even kill them.

🌡️

Climate Change

Warmer sand produces more female turtles. We need balance! Rising seas also wash away nesting beaches.

🌟 Simple Ways to Help

Every small action counts! Here's what YOU can do:

  • Reduce Plastic Use - Say no to plastic bags, straws, and bottles. Use reusable items instead!
  • Clean Up Beaches - Join or organize beach cleanups. Remove trash before it reaches the ocean!
  • Turn Off Lights - If you live near the beach, turn off outdoor lights during nesting season (May-October).
  • Give Turtles Space - If you see a nesting turtle, stay back and quiet. Flash photography and loud noises scare them!
  • Spread the Word - Tell your friends and family about turtles and why they need protection!
  • Support Conservation - Donate to turtle rescue organizations or symbolically adopt a sea turtle!
  • Be a Responsible Boater - Watch for turtles in the water and slow down in turtle zones!
  • Don't Buy Shell Products - Never buy tortoiseshell jewelry or items - it's illegal and hurts hawksbill turtles!

🐢 Remember!

Only 1 in 1,000 sea turtle hatchlings survive to adulthood. Every turtle counts, and every action you take makes a difference!
